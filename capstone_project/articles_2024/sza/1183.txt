Kanye West and Ty Dolla $ign's new joint album, Vultures 1, debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200. It's the first chart leader for Ty Dolla $ign and the 11th for West. The album earned 148.000 equivalent album units exclusively through streaming and digital sales. Physical versions of the record haven't been released yet.
Usher earned his highest-charting record since 2012 as Coming Home debuted in the No. 2 spot. Noah Kahan’s Stick Season climbed back to its peak in the No. 3 spot, while Morgan Wallen’s One Thing At A Time falls to No. 4 and SZA’s SOS dips to No. 5.
