The countdown is on to Lollapalooza. The festival will bring four days of music across eight stages to Grant Park in the heart of Chicago. More than 170 artists are set to take the stage. Thursday’s headliners will be Megan Thee Stallion and Hozier, joined by a lineup of acts including Labrinth and Kesha. Friday, headliners SZA and Stray Kids will take the stage, along with Victoria Monét and and Brandi Cyrus.
The Saturday lineup includes the Deftones, Killer Mike, and Infinity Song. The night’s headliners: The Killers and Future X Metro Boomin.
The festival’s final day includes Conan Gray, Vince Staples, and Carmen Delon with headliners Blink-182 and Melanie Martinez.
This year, festival organizers are taking a few steps toward sustainability. One big change-- Lollapalooza will be the first festival in the nation to have a main stage run entirely on a hybrid battery system, reports Billboard. Another change: the food. More than 30 food vendors are on the lineup this year, including two plant-based options: Plant Base and Vibe Village, reports Chicago Tribune. 
Lollapalooza basics:
Time: Lollapalooza is from 11 a.m.-10 p.m. Thursday, Aug. 1 to Sunday, Aug.4
Place: Lollapalooza will be at Grant Park 337 E. Randolph St. Chicago, IL 60601
