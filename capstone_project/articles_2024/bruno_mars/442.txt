She’s one of the greatest singer-songwriters of our generation. Needless to say, any music fan might be wondering how many Grammys Billie Eilish has in total. The “bad guy” singer has been consistently nominated for the prestigious awards and it’s very impressive to see how many of those golden gramophones she was given. 
Frequently collaborating with her brother FINNEAS, Eilish rose to fame with her song “Oceans Eyes” and skyrocketed to the top of the charts with her debut album When We All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go where it debuted as No. 1 on the Billboard 200. She’s written songs for movies like No Time To Die and Turning Red. Her second album Happier Than Ever was also critically acclaimed and also debuted on top of the Billboard 200.
So how many Grammys does Billie Eilish have? Read more to find out. 
Billie Eilish has nine Grammy Awards that she won through several years as an artist. She won Song Of The Year and Record Of The Year for “Bad Guy” and Best Pop Vocal Album Of The Year and Album Of The Year for When All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go. She also won Best New Artist that year as well. The following year she won Record of The Year for “Everything I Wanted” and Best Song Written For Visual Media for “No Time To Die.” However, she hasn’t won any Grammys the following two years.  
Billie Eilish won 5 Grammy Awards at the 62nd Grammy Awards. She won Song Of The Year and Record Of The Year for “Bad Guy” and Best Pop Vocal Album Of The Year and Album Of The Year for When All Fall Asleep, Where Do We Go. She also won Best New Artist, and became the youngest person to win all four major categories at the Grammys at 18 years old. 
Billie Eilish won 2 Grammys for Record of The Year for “Everything I Wanted” and Best Song Written For Visual Media for “No Time To Die.” She was also nominated for Best Pop Vocal Performance and Song Of The Year for “Everything I Wanted.” 
Billie Eilish did not win any Grammys at the 64th Annual Grammy Awards in 2022. She was nominated for Best Pop Solo Performance, Best Music Video, Song Of The Year, and Record of The Year for “Happier Than Ever.” Her album with the same name Happier Than Ever was nominated for Album Of The Year, and her music film that coincided with the release Happier Than Ever: A Love Letter To Los Angeles was nominated for Best Music Film  
Billie Eilish did not win any Grammy Awards at the 65th Grammy Awards. She was nominated for Best Song Written For Visual Media for “Nobody Like U” from the Disney animated film Turning Red, which she co-wrote with Finneas. She was also nominated for Best Music Film Billie Eilish Live At The O2.
Before the official televised ceremonies began, Billie Eilish won a Grammy for Best Song Made For Visual Media. She also won Song of the Year. She was nominated for her song “What Was I Made For?” that she wrote with FINNEAS for Barbie for several categories. The song is nominated for Record of The Year, Song of The Year, Best Pop Solo Performance, Best Song For Visual Media, and Best Music Video. 
If she wins Record Of The Year, she joins Bruno Mars and Paul Simon for the most wins in the category Record of The Year. 
Billie Eilish has been nominated for seven nominations for Song of the Year, Best Pop Vocal Album, Record of the Year, Album of the Year, Best Dance Pop Recording, and Best Pop Duo/Group Performance.
