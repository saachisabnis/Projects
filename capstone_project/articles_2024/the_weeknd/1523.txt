While Leap Day might not hold huge significance for most of us, it's a crucial day for anyone born on February 29, who technically only has a birthday once every four years.
People born on February 29 have to decide whether to celebrate their birthday on February 28 or March 1 - although it's a often joked that Leap Day babies are technically a quarter of their own age. 
It's not unheard off for celebrities to shave a few years off their age in order to appear younger, but Leap Year babies can jokingly claim they're doing this legitimately. 
So who are the famous faces born on February 29 and what are their Leap Year ages versus the real number of years they've been on this planet?
