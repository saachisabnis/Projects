PORTLAND, Ore. (KOIN) — April will be a month of music, with sibling groups, death metal bands and drag performers stopping in Portland.
Here is your guide to some of the top concerts scheduled for the month.
 OHSU, PSU open new center for behavioral health and aging 
When: Tuesday, Apr. 2 at 9 p.m.Where: Roseland Theater, 8 NW 6th Ave, Portland, OR 97209
Electronic duo Hippie Sabotage will perform tracks from its latest album “Trailblazer” and beyond. The brothers are best known for their remix of Tove Lo’s “Habits (Stay High)” and their 2016 single “Devil Eyes” that had a recent resurgence on TikTok.
When: Friday, Apr. 5 at 8 p.m.Where: Moda Center, 1 N Center Ct St, Portland, OR 97227
Mexican singer and producer Luis Miguel’s worldwide tour includes a stop at the Rose City’s biggest live music venue. The pop star’s previous tour set the record for the highest-grossing tour by a Latin artist, a record that has since been surpassed by Bad Bunny and Daddy Yankee.
When: Friday, Apr. 5 at 8 p.m.Where: McMenamins Crystal Ballroom, 1332 W Burnside St, Portland, OR 97209
Sleater-Kinney released its 11th studio album in January, and has already hit the road to sing it for audiences. The Olympia, Wash.-formed group told AP News its new record “Little Rope” focuses on loss and grief.
 Gov. Kotek signs bill to reverse Measure 110, recriminalizing small-quantity drug possession 
When: Saturday, Apr. 7 at 8 p.m.Where: Aladdin Theater, 3017 SE Milwaukie Ave, Portland, OR 9720
In 2021, The Veronicas returned to the music scene with their first album in seven years. The Australian sister duo hasn’t stopped since, with their recent “Gothic Summer” release debuting on Australia’s top 10 chart.
When: Friday, Apr. 12 at 8 p.m.Where: Revolution Hall, 1300 SE Stark St #203, Portland, OR 97214
Sasha Colby won the 15th season of “RuPaul’s Drag Race” in 2023, the same year she shared her debut single “Feel the Power.” The drag queen said the concert will be a “stripped down version of a show I always grew up loving.”
When: Wednesday, Apr. 25 at 7 p.m.Where: Moda Center, 1 N Center Ct St, Portland, OR 97227
Brothers Adam, Jack and Ryan Met make up AJR, best known for tracks “Bang!” and “Way Less Sad.” The indie pop trio is supporting their latest album “The Maybe Man” for their first-ever arena tour.
 Oregon bottle bill ‘not intended as cash for addicts’ 
When: Friday, Apr. 26 at 6:30 p.m.Where: Alaska Airlines’ Theater of the Clouds, 1016-1098 N Center Ct St, Portland, OR 97227
Swedish death metal band Amon Amarth is embarking on its “biggest North American headline tour to date.” The group will be joined fellow death metal groups Cannibal Corpse, Obituary and Frozen Soul.
