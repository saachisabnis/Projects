A Kansas City-based company has bought a music festival that previously has featured artists including Taylor Swift, Chris Stapleton, Miranda Lambert and Kenny Chesney.
Kustom Entertainment Inc., a wholly-owned subsidiary of Digital Ally Inc., acquired the Country Stampede music festival.

The outdoor country music festival is among the largest music festivals in the Midwest. Kustom, a live event marketing and concert production company, described the acquisition as a “significant milestone.”
Since 2019, the Country Stampede has been held in Topeka, but this year, it’s moving to the Azura Amphitheater in Bonner Springs from June 27-29. Musical acts will include Chris Janson, Riley Green and Jon Pardi, among others.
