The American Legion Chino Post Auxiliary 299 is seeking volunteers to help make paper poppies for its poppy project from 2 to 4 p.m. Wednesday, April 24 at the American Legion, 13759 Central Ave. in Chino.
Information: Renee Jones, (626) 893-3684.
National Shakespeare Day will be celebrated at the Chino Hills 55+ Club meeting at 10 a.m. Friday, April 26 at the Chino Hills Community Center, 14250 Peyton Drive. Coffee and registration starts at 9:30 a.m.
A presentation will take place on fun facts about Shakespeare.
A book sale for $1 per book will take place at the meeting.
Residents are encouraged to come early and browse.
The event is sponsored by the Book Club. Cash or checks will be accepted.
April birthdays and anniversaries will be recognized.
Members and guests are invited to meet for lunch after the meeting at
Chili’s Restaurant, 3670 Grand Ave., in Chino Hills.
Information:  Sharon Stuewe 909-226-8686.
Chino Hills residents interested in learning about summer recreation programs can attend an open house from 10 a.m. to 1 p.m. Saturday, April 27, at the Chino Hills Community Center, 14250 Peyton Drive.
Participants can watch class demonstrations, meet Community Services staff, and learn about summer programs offered by the city.
Crafts and activities will be available for families.
Information: Community Services, (909) 364-2700.
The St. Margaret Mary Knights of Columbus in Chino will host a fundraiser during business hours Monday, April 22 at Chino Hills Pizza Company, 14550 Pipeline Ave. in Chino.  A percentage of sales will benefit the club, which provides assistance to parishioners and community members in need.
The Knights of Columbus should be mentioned when placing an order at the restaurant.
Information: Gary George, (909) 957-3466.
The Chino Community Theatre will present Spamalot, based on the 1975 film “Monty Python and the Holy Grail,” at 2:30 and 8 p.m. today (April 20), 8 p.m.  April 26, and 27; and at 2:30 p.m. April 21, and 28 at 13123 Seventh St.
The musical offers an irreverent parody of the legend of King Arthur and his Knights of the Round Table featuring a bevy of beautiful show girls, cows, killer rabbits, and French people.
Director is Merrilee Drake.
Audience advisory: the play contains some risqué humor and adult situations.
General admission is $18.
Tickets for students and seniors 60-plus are $15.
For reservations, call the box office at (909) 590-1149 or visit chinocommunitytheatre.org and click on SeatYourself.
Bingo in the Barn, sponsored by the Chino Hills 55+ Club, is played every Thursday at the McCoy Equestrian Center, 14280 Peyton Drive.  Bingo sales begin at 10 a.m.
Games start at 11:30 a.m. and finish between 2 and 2:30 p.m. Minimum buy-in is $15 for all 15 games.  
Players must be at least 18 years old.
Proceeds benefit charitable causes in the community.
Information: (714) 345-0907.
Singers ages 50+ are wanted for the Chino Senior Chorus.  Rehearsals are held Tuesday mornings to prepare for performances at local senior living facilities.
Information: Richard Harris at (909) 597-4977.
