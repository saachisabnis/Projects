Music legend James Taylor will hit the road next year for a 2025 summer tour alongside his All-Star Band, playing 24 dates across the United States and Canada. The tour kicks off May 5 in Phoenix, Arizona, and wraps up July 1 in Gilford, New Hampshire.
Taylor’s 2025 tour will bring his signature sound to renowned venues such as Red Rocks Amphitheatre in Colorado, Chicago’s Ravinia Festival, and Toronto’s Budweiser Stage. The tour also features back-to-back nights at the Rady Shell in San Diego and the Santa Barbara Bowl.
Tickets go on sale to the general public on December 13 at 10 a.m. local time. Full tour details and ticketing information are available at tour.jamestaylor.com.
Boston-based trio Tiny Habits will join Taylor as the opening act. Praised by outlets like NPR and The New Yorker, Tiny Habits recently released their debut album All For Something. The group has supported acts like Gracie Abrams, Noah Kahan, and Vance Joy.
Taylor, a six-time GRAMMY winner and member of both the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and the Songwriters Hall of Fame, has been a beloved fixture in the music world for over five decades. Known for his soothing baritone and masterful guitar work, Taylor has sold more than 100 million albums worldwide since his self-titled 1968 debut.
Over his storied career, Taylor has received numerous accolades, including the Presidential Medal of Freedom, Kennedy Center Honors, and the National Medal of Arts. His 19th studio album, American Standard, earned him the 2021 GRAMMY Award for Best Traditional Pop Vocal Album.
Fans eager to experience Taylor’s timeless catalog and captivating live performances can look forward to an evening filled with classics and new favorites.
