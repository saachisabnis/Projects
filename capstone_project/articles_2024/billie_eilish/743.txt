Pop sensation Billie Eilish is making waves in music and environmental activism thanks to her latest collaboration with Google Maps. Eilish’s upcoming “Hit Me Hard and Soft” tour, which kicks off on September 29 in Quebec City, is set to be a trailblazer in promoting sustainability among her fans.Source: Vogue/YouTubeGoogle Maps will play a pivotal role during the 43-city world tour by helping concert-goers locate eco-friendly transportation options and plant-based dining. Fans looking to minimize their environmental footprint can rely on Google Maps to suggest fuel-efficient routes, walking, bike-sharing, and public transport alternatives. For those attending concerts in cities like Atlanta, Baltimore, Chicago, Detroit, and Los Angeles, among others, Eilish has personally recommended top spots for plant-based meals, easily accessible via the app.Eilish’s commitment to sustainability extends beyond just transportation and diet. The partnership aims to enhance fans’ awareness and choices, aligning with the artist’s long-standing advocacy for environmental causes. “I am so excited to see you all at my shows over the coming months, and for us to work together in reducing our collective footprint,” Eilish expressed in a recent statement. She emphasized the importance of every small action in contributing to the planet’s health.The initiative is supported by Eilish’s mother, Maggie Baird, who is also the founder of Support + Feed—an organization that promotes access to plant-based foods.
