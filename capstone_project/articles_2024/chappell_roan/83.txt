Sabrina Carpenter’s Short n’ Sweet remains on top of the Billboard 200 chart for a second week, after debuting at No. 1 a week ago. Short n’ Sweet had a big second week, with the album earning 159,000 equivalent album units in the U.S. Only three other albums have topped Carpenter’s second week sales total In the past 12 months: Taylor Swift’s The Tortured Poets Department tallied 439,000 units, Swift’s 1989 (Taylor’s Version) earned 245,000, and Drake’s For All the Dogs moved 164,000. Two notable debuts reached the Billboard 200 Top 10 this week, as LE SSERAFIM’s CRAZY opens at No. 7, while Destroy Lonely’s Love Lasts Forever enters at No. 10. 
The rest of the Billboard 200 Top 5 is filled out by Post Malone’s F-1 Trillion moving up one spot to No. 2, Chappell Roan’s The Rise and Fall of a Midwest Princess rising up a slot to No. 3, Morgan Wallen’s One Thing at a Time jumping up at spot to No. 4, and Swift’s former No. 1 The Tortured Poets Department climbing up one spot to return to the Top 5 at No. 5. (Billboard)
