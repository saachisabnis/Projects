Steve Lukather, left, and Joseph Williams of Toto will be on tour with the classic rock band in 2025.(Courtesy photo/Masanori Doi)
Classic rock fans in Birmingham will want to mark their calendars for July 21, 2025. Three vintage music acts will be in town that night, ready to perform 1970s and ‘80s hits at the new Coca-Cola Amphitheater.
Toto, Christopher Cross and Men at Work (including iconic frontman Colin Hay) will be on tour together next year, traveling to nearly 30 cities across the United States. Their show in Birmingham, which starts at 6:45 p.m., is the trio’s only stop in Alabama next year, according to the current tour schedule. (See the full tour agenda here.)
Tickets for the Birmingham show go on sale Friday, Dec. 13, at 10 a.m. CT via Ticketmaster/Live Nation, after a round of pre-sales. Prices are $39.50, $59.50, $79.50, $99.50 and $129.50, plus service charges, according to promoter Live Nation.
Pre-sales start on Wednesday, Dec. 11, at 10 a.m. CT and end on Thursday, Dec. 12, at 11:59 p.m. CT, the ticket listing says.
The Coca-Cola Amphitheater, a $46 million concert venue, is under construction and set to be completed in June 2025, according to organizers. The 9,380-seat amphitheater already has announced several concert dates for its debut season, with shows by Jason Isbell, Nelly, Styx, Rod Stewart, Matt Rife, Riley Green, Thomas Rhett and Teddy Swims.

Toto will be touring in 2025, with a lineup that includes founding member Steve Lukather, fourth from left.(Courtesy photo/Michelle Brody)
Toto, formed in 1977 in Los Angeles, earned fame with albums such as “Toto” (1978), “Hydra” (1979) and “Toto IV” (1982), along with hit singles such as “Hold the Line,” “Rosanna,” “Africa” and “I Won’t Hold You Back.” Toto has three Grammy wins to its credit, including Album of the Year for “Toto IV” and Record of the Year for “Rosanna,” both in 1983.
Founding member Steve Lukather, a guitarist and singer, remain with the band, and will be touring with Toto in 2025, along with longtime singer Joseph Williams. The touring lineup also includes keyboard player Greg Phillinganes, drummer Shannon Forrest, bassist John Pierce, horn player and percussionist Warren Ham and keyboard player Dennis Atlas.
Christopher Cross performs at soundcheck for "The Music Of Paul McCartney" at City Winery on March 14, 2023, in New York City. He'll be on tour with Toto and Men at Work in 2025.(Photo by Al Pereira/Michael Ochs Archives/Getty Images)
Christopher Cross, a singer-songwriter and guitarist, is best known for his self-titled debut album, released in 1979, and the hit singles “Sailing,” “Ride Like the Wind” and “Never Be the Same.” His album won five Grammy Awards in 1982, including Album of the Year, Song of the Year and Record of the Year (both for “Sailing”), and Best New Artist.
Cross also earned acclaim for the No. 1 single “Arthur’s Theme (Best That You Can Do),” from the soundtrack of the 1981 movie “Arthur.” He won an Oscar and a Golden Globe for “Arthur’s Theme” in 1982, both in the category of Best Original Song.
Colin Hay of Men At Work performs at the Viña del Mar International Song Festival  on Feb. 28, 2024, in Chile. He'll be on tour in 2025 with Men at Work, Toto and Christopher Cross.(Photo by Lucas Aguayo/Getty Images)
Men at Work, formed in Australia in 1979, earned fame in the United States during the early 1980s, with albums such as “Business as Usual” (1981) and “Cargo” (1982), and breakthrough hits such as “Down Under,” “Who Can It Be Now?” and “Overkill.” Men at Work won the Grammy for Best New Artist in 1983. 
The band’s founding frontman, singer-guitarist Colin Hay, will be touring with Men at Work in 2025, on a lineup that features drummer Jimmy Branly, bassist Yosmel Montejo, guitarist San Miguel, percussionist Cecilia Noël and multi-instrumentalist Scheila Gonzalez.
