Justin Bieber is a Canadian singer, songwriter, and pop icon who gained international fame at a young age. Born on March 1, 1994, in London, Ontario, Bieber was discovered through his YouTube channel by talent manager Scooter Braun. He quickly rose to prominence in the late 2000s with his debut EP, “My World,” and his debut studio album, “My World 2.0,” which featured hit singles like “Baby.”
Bieber’s music is characterized by its catchy melodies, smooth vocals, and contemporary pop sound. He has released several successful albums throughout his career, including “Believe” (2012), “Purpose” (2015), and “Changes” (2020). His music often explores themes of love, relationships, and personal growth, reflecting his own experiences as he navigates fame and adulthood.
In addition to his music career, Bieber is known for his high-profile relationships, public controversies, and philanthropic efforts. He has won numerous awards, including Grammy Awards, American Music Awards, and MTV Video Music Awards, and has sold millions of records worldwide.
Bieber’s impact on popular culture is undeniable, and he remains one of the most influential and recognizable figures in contemporary music. With his talent, charisma, and dedicated fanbase, he continues to push the boundaries of pop music and inspire audiences around the world.

“Sorry” is a song by Justin Bieber, released in 2015 as the second single from his fourth studio album, “Purpose.” The song is characterized by its infectious tropical house beat, catchy melody, and Bieber’s smooth vocals.

It received praise for its catchy hooks, polished production, and Bieber’s matured vocal delivery.
