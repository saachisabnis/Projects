Mob James, in a recent interview with Reggie Wright Jr., opened up about the exclusive audience at Kendrick Lamar's "Pop Out" concert. According to Mob James, the crowd was predominantly affluent individuals, referencing high-profile attendees such as LeBron James. The conversation also touched on the misconceptions surrounding the event. Some believed Kendrick Lamar was making a statement about gang unity in Los Angeles. Mob James clarified that while Kendrick wasn't necessarily promoting gang truces, he showcased that Bloods and Crips could co-exist peacefully in shared spaces. He emphasized the importance of unity, highlighting how the event brought together different groups without conflict, reinforcing that collaboration and peaceful interactions are possible despite gang affiliations.
Interview by: Reggie Wright Jr.
