The Summer Smash 2024, presented by Lyrical Lemonade, once again took SeatGeek Stadium by storm from June 14th to June 16th. This year's festival featured an impressive lineup, and as Nuvo Indy celebrates our third year covering this vibrant event, we're thrilled to share why Summer Smash remains our favorite festival in the Midwest.
 Day 1: A Star-Studded Kickoff
The festival's first day set the bar high with headliner Cactus Jack, featuring the iconic Travis Scott, delivering a high-energy performance that had the crowd roaring. The stage was graced by a stellar lineup, including Big Sean, who brought his Detroit flair, and the bold and charismatic Flo Milli. That Mexican OT and BKTHERULA also left lasting impressions with their dynamic sets.
Emerging talents like Black Kray and BLP Kosher provided fresh sounds, while Famous Dex brought nostalgia with his hit tracks. The day also saw captivating performances from CLIP and Lazer Dim 700, who energized the audience with their unique styles.
 Day 2: Playboi Carti Leads the Charge
Day two was headlined by the enigmatic Playboi Carti, whose electrifying set had fans raving. The lineup continued to impress with Kodak Black and Lil Tecca delivering memorable performances. Ski Mask the Slump God and Lil Skies brought their distinctive flows, captivating the audience.
Mick Jenkins performed "The Water[s]," a deep and introspective set that resonated with long-time fans. Rising stars like Rich Amiri and Paris Texas showcased their talent, hinting at bright futures ahead. The day also featured high-energy acts like Baby KIA and Cash Cobain, ensuring the momentum never faltered.
 Day 3: Chief Keef Closes with a Bang
The final day of Summer Smash 2024 was nothing short of spectacular, with Chief Keef leading the charge. His performance was a highlight, celebrating his impact on the hip-hop scene. The lineup featured heavy hitters like Denzel Curry and JID, whose lyrical prowess and stage presence were undeniable.
Ken Carson, Lil Yachty, and Waka Flocka Flame kept the energy high, while artists like Anycia and BabyTron showcased their unique styles. The diverse lineup continued with acts like Joeyy and Lil Gnar, who ensured the festival's closing day was unforgettable.
The festival also featured incredible DJ sets, with Zack Bia, Kerwin Frost, Filthy, DJ Scheme, and GloupJake keeping the beats flowing between performances.
Final Thoughts
Summer Smash 2024 has once again proven why it is the premier hip-hop festival in the Midwest. With a lineup that seamlessly blends established stars and emerging talents, the festival offered a dynamic and unforgettable experience. 
At Nuvo Indy, we are honored to have covered this festival for the third year in a row, and we can confidently say that Summer Smash is more than just an event—it's a celebration of music, culture, and community. We look forward to next year's edition and can't wait to bring you more highlights from our favorite Midwest festival.
