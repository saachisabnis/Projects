Nebraska, known for its vast prairies, rolling hills, and vibrant music scene, has produced a diverse array of talented singers who have made their mark on various genres, from country and folk to rock and pop. These artists have not only showcased the Cornhusker State’s musical prowess but have also left an indelible impact on the global music landscape. In this article, we’ll explore the top 10 famous singers from Nebraska and delve into their remarkable careers and the contributions they have made to the world of music.

Marlon Brando, born in Omaha, Nebraska, is best known for his iconic acting career, but he was also a talented singer. Brando’s musical abilities were showcased in several of his films, such as “Guys and Dolls” and “The Teahouse of the August Moon.” His deep, resonant voice and his ability to convey emotion through his performances made him a captivating presence on both stage and screen. Brando’s singing style was heavily influenced by his love of jazz and blues, and he often incorporated elements of these genres into his performances. In addition to his film work, Brando also released several albums, including “Marlon Brando Sings” and “Marlon Brando: the Wild One,” which featured covers of popular songs as well as original compositions. Although his musical career was overshadowed by his acting achievements, Brando’s contributions to the world of music and his status as a cultural icon have made him one of Nebraska’s most famous and influential exports.
Conor Oberst, born in Omaha, Nebraska, is a singer-songwriter best known as the lead vocalist and guitarist of the indie rock band Bright Eyes. With his emotive vocals, poetic lyrics, and genre-bending sound, Oberst has become one of the most influential and acclaimed artists in the world of alternative music. His music often explores themes of love, loss, politics, and personal struggles, delivered with a raw, confessional honesty that has earned him a devoted following. Throughout his career, Oberst has released numerous critically acclaimed albums, both as a solo artist and with Bright Eyes, showcasing his evolution as a songwriter and his ability to continually push the boundaries of his craft. In addition to his work with Bright Eyes, Oberst has also collaborated with various other artists and has been involved in several side projects, including Desaparecidos and The Mystic Valley Band. As a native of Nebraska and a leading figure in the indie music scene, Conor Oberst’s contributions to the world of alternative rock have made him one of the state’s most celebrated and influential musical exports.
Lady Gaga, born Stefani Joanne Angelina Germanotta in New York City, spent part of her childhood in Omaha, Nebraska. Known for her powerful vocals, theatrical performances, and boundary-pushing fashion choices, Lady Gaga has become one of the most successful and influential pop artists of her generation. With a career spanning over a decade, she has released numerous chart-topping albums and singles, showcasing her versatility as a singer, songwriter, and musician. Lady Gaga’s music often celebrates individuality, self-expression, and LGBTQ+ rights, and her performances are known for their elaborate staging and choreography. Beyond her musical achievements, Lady Gaga has also made a name for herself as an actress, receiving critical acclaim for her roles in “A Star Is Born” and “American Horror Story.” Throughout her career, she has been recognized with numerous awards and accolades, including multiple Grammy Awards and an Academy Award. Although not a native Nebraskan, Lady Gaga’s time spent in Omaha has contributed to the state’s rich musical heritage and has helped to inspire a new generation of aspiring artists.
Matthew Sweet, born in Lincoln, Nebraska, is a singer-songwriter and multi-instrumentalist known for his power pop and alternative rock sound. With his jangly guitars, infectious melodies, and introspective lyrics, Sweet has become a beloved figure in the world of indie music. His breakthrough album, “Girlfriend,” released in 1991, showcased his talent for crafting catchy, emotionally resonant songs and cemented his status as a leading voice in the alternative rock scene. Throughout his career, Sweet has released numerous critically acclaimed albums that have explored various musical styles, from folk and country to experimental rock. He has also collaborated with a wide range of artists, including The Bangles, Susanna Hoffs, and Shawn Mullins. In addition to his work as a solo artist, Sweet has been involved in several side projects and has contributed music to films and television shows. As a native of Nebraska and a pioneering figure in the world of alternative music, Matthew Sweet’s contributions to the indie rock landscape have made him one of the state’s most influential and respected musical exports.
Elliott Smith, born Steven Paul Smith in Omaha, Nebraska, was a singer-songwriter and multi-instrumentalist known for his intimate, introspective music and his distinctive fingerpicking guitar style. With his whisper-soft vocals, poetic lyrics, and melancholic melodies, Smith became a cult figure in the indie music scene, earning critical acclaim and a devoted following. His albums, such as “Either/Or” and “XO,” showcased his talent for crafting deeply personal, emotionally raw songs that explored themes of love, loss, addiction, and mental health. Smith’s music often blended elements of folk, rock, and pop, creating a unique and captivating sound that set him apart from his contemporaries. In addition to his solo work, Smith collaborated with various artists and contributed music to films, including “Good Will Hunting,” for which he received an Academy Award nomination. Tragically, Smith’s life and career were cut short in 2003 when he died by suicide at the age of 34. Despite his untimely death, his legacy as a gifted songwriter and a pioneering figure in the indie music scene continues to inspire and influence countless artists. As a native of Nebraska, Elliott Smith’s contributions to the world of music have made him one of the state’s most beloved and enduring cultural icons.
Randy Meisner, born in Scottsbluff, Nebraska, is a singer, songwriter, and bassist best known as a founding member of the iconic rock band The Eagles. With his high, clear vocals and his talent for crafting melodic basslines, Meisner played a key role in shaping the band’s signature sound and in creating some of their most beloved songs, including “Take It to the Limit” and “Try and Love Again.” Prior to his work with The Eagles, Meisner was a member of several notable bands, including Poco and Rick Nelson’s Stone Canyon Band, where he honed his skills as a musician and songwriter. Throughout his career, Meisner has also pursued a successful solo career, releasing several albums that showcase his talents as a lead vocalist and his ability to blend elements of country, rock, and folk into his music. Despite facing personal and health challenges, Meisner’s contributions to the world of rock music and his status as a founding member of one of the most successful and influential bands of all time have cemented his place in music history. As a native of Nebraska, Randy Meisner’s musical legacy and his role in shaping the sound of American rock have made him one of the state’s most significant and celebrated cultural figures.
Lyle Lovett, born in Klein, Texas, and raised in Houston, Texas, spent part of his childhood in Greenfield, Iowa, has family ties to Nebraska, and has referenced the state in his music. He is a singer, songwriter, and actor known for his eclectic blend of country, folk, swing, and blues, as well as his witty, observational lyrics. With his distinctive voice and his talent for crafting clever, often irreverent songs, Lovett has become one of the most respected and beloved figures in the world of Americana music. Throughout his career, he has released numerous critically acclaimed albums, showcasing his versatility as a songwriter and his ability to seamlessly blend various musical genres. Lovett’s music often explores themes of love, heartbreak, and the complexities of human relationships, delivered with a mix of humor, poignancy, and insight. In addition to his work as a musician, Lovett has also pursued a successful acting career, appearing in films such as “The Player” and “Short Cuts,” and on television shows like “Mad About You” and “Castle.” Although not a Nebraska native, Lyle Lovett’s connections to the state and his contributions to the world of Americana music have helped to enrich the state’s cultural heritage.
Wynonie Harris, born in Omaha, Nebraska, was a rhythm and blues singer known for his powerful, gravelly voice and his energetic, often risqué performances. Considered one of the forerunners of rock and roll, Harris’s music blended elements of blues, jump blues, and R&B, creating a raw, infectious sound that electrified audiences and influenced countless musicians. Throughout his career, Harris released numerous hit singles, including “Good Rockin’ Tonight,” “All She Wants to Do Is Rock,” and “Bloodshot Eyes,” which showcased his dynamic vocal style and his talent for delivering suggestive, often humorous lyrics. His live performances were legendary for their intensity and showmanship, with Harris often incorporating athletic moves and playful interactions with the audience into his act. Despite facing personal struggles and a decline in popularity in his later years, Harris’s contributions to the development of rhythm and blues and his influence on the birth of rock and roll have earned him a place in music history. As a native of Nebraska and a pioneering figure in the world of R&B, Wynonie Harris’s musical legacy has made him one of the state’s most significant and influential cultural figures.
Buddy Miles, born George Allen Miles Jr. in Omaha, Nebraska, was a drummer, vocalist, and songwriter known for his powerful, soulful singing and his innovative drumming style. Throughout his career, Miles collaborated with some of the most iconic musicians of his generation, including Jimi Hendrix, Carlos Santana, and Stevie Wonder, and made significant contributions to the development of rock, funk, and soul music. As a member of Hendrix’s Band of Gypsys, Miles showcased his talents as a vocalist and drummer, delivering electrifying performances on songs like “Machine Gun” and “Power of Soul.” He also released several successful solo albums, including “Them Changes,” which featured his signature blend of rock, funk, and R&B. Miles’s music often explored themes of social and political commentary, delivered with a raw, passionate intensity that reflected his commitment to using his art as a force for change. Throughout his career, he faced personal and professional challenges, including struggles with addiction, but his contributions to the world of music and his influence on countless artists have ensured his place in rock history. As a native of Nebraska and a pioneering figure in the development of funk and soul, Buddy Miles’s musical legacy has made him one of the state’s most significant and influential cultural exports.
Jaime Wyatt, born in Grand Island, Nebraska, is a country singer and songwriter known for her raw, honest lyrics and her blend of traditional and contemporary country influences. With her powerful, expressive vocals and her talent for crafting deeply personal, often autobiographical songs, Wyatt has become a rising star in the world of Americana music. Her debut album, “Felony Blues,” released in 2017, showcased her ability to channel her own experiences with addiction, incarceration, and redemption into compelling, emotionally resonant music. Wyatt’s songs often explore themes of struggle, perseverance, and the search for meaning and purpose in life, delivered with a mix of grit, vulnerability, and hope. In addition to her solo work, Wyatt has collaborated with various artists and has been a sought-after songwriter in the Nashville music scene. Her music has been praised for its authenticity, its emotional depth, and its ability to connect with listeners on a profound level. As a native of Nebraska and a rising talent in the world of country and Americana music, Jaime Wyatt’s contributions to the genre and her powerful, inspiring personal story have made her one of the state’s most promising and influential young artists.
