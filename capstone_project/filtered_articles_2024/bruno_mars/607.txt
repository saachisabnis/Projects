Hawaii, a melting pot of cultures nestled in the heart of the Pacific Ocean, has long been celebrated for its breathtaking landscapes, rich history, and vibrant cultural tapestry. Beyond its sandy shores and lush tropical forests, Hawaii boasts a dynamic music scene that reflects the diverse influences of its people and their stories. This article shines a spotlight on the Top 10 Famous Singers from Hawaii, artists who have transcended the islands’ boundaries to make a significant impact on the global music stage. From the soulful melodies of traditional Hawaiian music to the contemporary beats that blend genres and cultures, these singers embody the spirit and heart of Hawaii. They have navigated the complexities of the music industry, bringing the aloha spirit to audiences worldwide, and in doing so, have become cultural ambassadors of their homeland. These artists, with their unique sounds and compelling stories, have not only contributed to the rich musical heritage of Hawaii but have also played a pivotal role in shaping the global perception of Hawaiian music. Through their artistry, they celebrate the complexities of Hawaiian identity, drawing from its past while innovating and pushing boundaries. This article delves into the lives and legacies of these remarkable musicians, exploring how their Hawaiian roots have influenced their music and how they have used their platform to promote Hawaiian culture on the world stage. From iconic figures who have paved the way for future generations to contemporary artists making waves today, each singer’s journey is a testament to the power of music to connect, inspire, and transcend.

Bruno Mars, a globally renowned singer and songwriter, hails from the beautiful island of Hawaii. Born Peter Gene Hernandez on October 8, 1985, in Honolulu, Mars has captivated audiences worldwide with his irresistible blend of pop, R&B, funk, and soul. With his smooth vocals, electrifying stage presence, and impressive musical versatility, he has cemented his status as one of the top artists of his generation. Mars burst onto the music scene with his debut album “Doo-Wops & Hooligans” in 2010, featuring hit singles like “Just the Way You Are” and “Grenade,” which earned him widespread acclaim and multiple Grammy Awards. Since then, he has continued to dominate the charts with chart-topping albums like “Unorthodox Jukebox” and “24K Magic,” showcasing his exceptional talent as a singer, songwriter, and performer.
Beyond his musical talents, Bruno Mars is celebrated for his dynamic live performances, often drawing comparisons to legendary entertainers like Michael Jackson and Prince. His infectious charisma and effortless charm have endeared him to millions of fans around the globe, making him a true icon in the music industry. With numerous awards, including Grammy Awards, Billboard Music Awards, and American Music Awards, Bruno Mars continues to push the boundaries of contemporary music while proudly representing his Hawaiian roots on the world stage.
Bette Midler, a beloved singer, actress, and comedian, traces her roots back to the picturesque landscapes of Hawaii. Born on December 1, 1945, in Honolulu, Midler’s journey to stardom is marked by her exceptional talent and versatile performances across various entertainment mediums. Often referred to as “The Divine Miss M,” she rose to prominence in the 1970s with her distinctive blend of powerhouse vocals, theatrical flair, and comedic wit. Midler’s debut album, “The Divine Miss M,” earned her a Grammy Award for Best New Artist and showcased her ability to captivate audiences with her soulful renditions of classic tunes and heartfelt ballads.
Throughout her illustrious career, Bette Midler has continued to enchant audiences with her magnetic stage presence and unparalleled versatility. From her iconic roles in films like “The Rose” and “Beaches” to her Tony Award-winning Broadway performances, she has left an indelible mark on the entertainment industry. With a string of chart-topping albums and countless accolades, including multiple Grammy Awards, Golden Globe Awards, and Emmy Awards, Midler remains a cherished icon whose contributions to music and entertainment transcend generations. Her enduring legacy as one of Hawaii’s most celebrated exports is a testament to her enduring talent and enduring appeal.
Israel Kamakawiwoʻole, affectionately known as “Iz,” is an iconic figure in Hawaiian music whose legacy continues to resonate across the world. Born on May 20, 1959, in Honolulu, Hawaii, Iz was a gifted singer, musician, and songwriter who captured the hearts of audiences with his soulful voice and heartfelt performances. He gained international acclaim for his hauntingly beautiful rendition of “Over the Rainbow” and “What a Wonderful World,” which blended traditional Hawaiian melodies with contemporary sounds. Iz’s distinctive voice, coupled with his ukulele playing, evoked a deep sense of nostalgia and longing, earning him a special place in the hearts of music lovers everywhere.
Despite his untimely passing in 1997, Israel Kamakawiwoʻole’s music continues to inspire and uplift listeners around the globe. His legacy as a cultural ambassador for Hawaii endures through his timeless melodies and profound lyrics, which celebrate the beauty of his homeland and the spirit of aloha. With his larger-than-life presence and enduring influence, Iz remains a symbol of Hawaii’s rich musical heritage and a beloved icon whose music transcends boundaries of time and culture.
Jake Shimabukuro is a virtuoso ukulele player whose remarkable talent has propelled him to international fame from his Hawaiian roots. Born on November 3, 1976, in Honolulu, Hawaii, Shimabukuro has redefined the possibilities of the ukulele, showcasing its versatility and power as a solo instrument. With his innovative playing techniques and captivating performances, he has captivated audiences worldwide, earning widespread acclaim from both music critics and fans alike. Shimabukuro’s mastery of the ukulele transcends genres, seamlessly blending elements of jazz, rock, blues, and traditional Hawaiian music into his own unique style.
Since emerging onto the music scene in the late 1990s, Jake Shimabukuro has released a string of critically acclaimed albums, including his breakout hit “Dragon” and the mesmerizing “Peace Love Ukulele,” which showcase his extraordinary skill and artistry. His electrifying live performances have earned him a reputation as one of the most exciting and dynamic performers in the world of music. Beyond his musical accomplishments, Shimabukuro is also recognized for his philanthropic work, using his platform to support various charitable causes and inspire the next generation of musicians. With his boundless creativity and infectious passion for music, Jake Shimabukuro continues to push the boundaries of what is possible on the ukulele, leaving an indelible mark on the world of music and representing Hawaii’s rich musical heritage with pride.
Jack Johnson, a beloved singer-songwriter, and musician, originates from the picturesque landscapes of Hawaii. Born on May 18, 1975, in Honolulu, Johnson’s laid-back acoustic melodies and introspective lyrics have garnered him a devoted global following. His music, characterized by its breezy melodies and thoughtful reflections on life and love, resonates with audiences of all ages. Johnson first gained widespread recognition with his debut album “Brushfire Fairytales” in 2001, featuring the hit single “Flake,” which showcased his signature blend of folk, rock, and surf music.
Throughout his career, Jack Johnson has remained true to his Hawaiian roots, often infusing his music with elements of island life and culture. Songs like “Banana Pancakes” and “Better Together” evoke the spirit of aloha, spreading a message of positivity and unity. In addition to his musical pursuits, Johnson is also an avid environmental activist, using his platform to raise awareness about sustainability and conservation efforts. With his soulful voice and heartfelt lyrics, Jack Johnson continues to inspire audiences around the world while proudly representing the rich musical heritage of Hawaii.
Tia Carrere, a versatile talent, was born on January 2, 1967, in Honolulu, Hawaii. While widely recognized for her acting career, particularly for her roles in films like “Wayne’s World” and “True Lies,” Carrere is also a talented singer with roots tracing back to Hawaii. She showcased her musical abilities in the 1990s with the release of her debut album “Dream,” featuring a blend of pop and rock influences. Carrere’s sultry vocals and captivating stage presence earned her critical acclaim, demonstrating her versatility beyond the silver screen. Despite her success in acting, Carrere’s passion for music has remained evident throughout her career, with occasional performances and contributions to soundtracks showcasing her multifaceted talents. As an artist with ties to Hawaii, Tia Carrere represents the diverse creative landscape of the islands, leaving an indelible mark on both the entertainment industry and the world of music.
Glenn Medeiros, a talented singer-songwriter, was born on June 24, 1970, in Lihue, Hawaii. Rising to prominence in the late 1980s, Medeiros captivated audiences with his smooth vocals and heartfelt ballads. His breakout hit, “Nothing’s Gonna Change My Love for You,” propelled him to international stardom, topping charts around the world and solidifying his status as a teen idol. Medeiros continued to release a string of successful albums, showcasing his musical versatility and distinctive voice. Despite stepping back from the spotlight in later years, his contributions to the music industry endure, with his timeless hits remaining beloved classics. As a proud ambassador of Hawaiian talent, Glenn Medeiros represents the rich musical heritage of the islands, leaving an enduring legacy that continues to resonate with fans worldwide.
Anuhea, born Rylee Anuheakeʻalaokalokelani Jenkins, is a Hawaiian singer-songwriter whose captivating voice and soulful melodies have earned her widespread acclaim. Hailing from Maui, Anuhea infuses her music with the spirit of the islands, blending elements of reggae, pop, and acoustic folk to create a uniquely Hawaiian sound. Her debut album, “Anuhea,” released in 2009, showcased her talent for crafting catchy hooks and heartfelt lyrics, earning her a dedicated fan base both in Hawaii and beyond. Anuhea’s music often explores themes of love, empowerment, and island life, resonating with listeners of all ages.
Beyond her musical endeavors, Anuhea is also known for her philanthropic work and advocacy for environmental conservation, reflecting her deep connection to her Hawaiian heritage. With her warm vocals and infectious energy, Anuhea continues to enchant audiences with her live performances, captivating listeners with her soulful voice and engaging stage presence. As a proud representative of Hawaiian culture, Anuhea’s music serves as a celebration of the beauty and diversity of the islands, inspiring audiences to embrace the aloha spirit wherever they may be.
Kealiʻi Reichel, a revered figure in Hawaiian music and culture, is renowned for his soul-stirring vocals and profound connection to his heritage. Born on May 24, 1962, in Maui, Hawaii, Reichel has dedicated his life to preserving and perpetuating traditional Hawaiian music and hula. As a singer, songwriter, and kumu hula (hula teacher), he has garnered international acclaim for his evocative performances, which showcase the beauty and depth of Hawaiian chants and melodies. Reichel’s groundbreaking albums, such as “Kawaipunahele” and “Melelana,” have earned him numerous awards and accolades, solidifying his legacy as one of Hawaii’s most influential cultural ambassadors.
Beyond his musical achievements, Kealiʻi Reichel is revered for his commitment to Hawaiian language revitalization and cultural education. Through his work as a teacher and advocate, he has inspired countless individuals to reconnect with their roots and embrace the traditions of their ancestors. Reichel’s unwavering dedication to preserving Hawaiian culture serves as a beacon of hope and pride for future generations, ensuring that the spirit of aloha continues to thrive for years to come. As a master storyteller and guardian of Hawaiian heritage, Kealiʻi Reichel’s impact on music and culture transcends borders, leaving an indelible mark on the world stage.
Napua Greig, a celebrated Hawaiian vocalist and cultural ambassador, hails from the verdant landscapes of Maui, Hawaii. Born into a family deeply rooted in Hawaiian music and hula, Greig has dedicated her life to honoring and perpetuating the traditions of her ancestors. Renowned for her exquisite vocals and profound understanding of Hawaiian chants and melodies, she has captivated audiences around the world with her evocative performances. Greig’s reverence for her cultural heritage is evident in her music, which serves as a powerful expression of the beauty and spirit of Hawaii.
In addition to her musical pursuits, Napua Greig is also a respected kumu hula (hula teacher) and advocate for Hawaiian language revitalization. Through her work, she seeks to instill a sense of pride and connection to Hawaiian culture among future generations. Greig’s commitment to preserving and sharing her heritage has earned her widespread admiration and numerous awards, solidifying her status as a leading figure in the Hawaiian cultural renaissance. With her soulful voice and unwavering dedication to perpetuating the traditions of her ancestors, Napua Greig continues to inspire and uplift audiences, ensuring that the spirit of aloha endures for generations to come.
